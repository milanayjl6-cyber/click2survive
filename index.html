Physics Reference

Governing equations, constants and assumptions used by each simulation. Written so a physics teacher can audit them.

Grade 9 — Buoyancy Laboratory (computed live)

F_b = ρ·g·V_displaced  ·  W = (m_hull + n·m_person)·g
floats ⟺ V_needed = W/(ρ·g) ≤ V_hull  ·  freeboard = 1 − V_needed/V_hull

Constants: g = 9.81 m/s² · ρ fresh = 1000 kg/m³ · ρ sea = 1025 kg/m³.
Assumptions: incompressible fluid, rigid hull, uniform loading, calm water, no dynamic waves.

Grades 7–8 — Floodwater thresholds (scenario classification)

F_drag = ½·ρ·C_d·A·v²  ·  F_hydrostatic ≈ ρ·g·h·A

Depth: <0.3 m safe · 0.3–0.5 m risky · >0.5 m unsafe (FEMA/UNDRR). Velocity: <0.5 m/s safe · 0.5–1.0 risky · >1.0 unsafe (Jonkman; UNSW WRL). Rainfall bands per PAGASA: Yellow 7.5–15, Orange 15–30, Red >30 mm/hr. These scenarios classify against published thresholds; the drag values shown in explanations are order-of-magnitude estimates for a standing adult (A ≈ 0.4 m², C_d ≈ 1.1).

Grade 10 — Wind on a person (scenario classification)

F_wind = ½·ρ_air·C_d·A·v²  (ρ_air = 1.2 kg/m³)

Wind bands per PAGASA/WMO: <60 km/h manageable · 60–100 strong · >100 dangerous.
